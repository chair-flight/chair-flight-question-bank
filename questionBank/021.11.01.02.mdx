# Design, types and components of turbine engines

## Learning Objectives

1. [021.11.01.02.01] -- List the main components of a basic gas turbine engine:
   inlet; compressor; combustion chamber; turbine; outlet.
2. [021.11.01.02.02] -- Describe the variation of static pressure, temperature
   and axial velocity in a gas turbine engine under normal operating conditions
   and with the aid of a working cycle diagram.
3. [021.11.01.02.03] -- Describe the differences between absolute,
   circumferential (tangential) and axial velocity.
4. [021.11.01.02.04] -- List the different types of gas turbine engines:
   straight jet; turbofan; turboprop.
5. [021.11.01.02.05] -- State that a gas turbine engine can have one or more
   spools.
6. [021.11.01.02.06] -- Describe how thrust is produced by turbojet and turbofan
   engines.
7. [021.11.01.02.07] -- Describe how power is produced by turboprop engines.
8. [021.11.01.02.08] -- Describe the term "equivalent horsepower" (= thrust
   horsepower + shaft horsepower).
9. [021.11.01.02.09] -- Explain the principle of a free turbine or free-power
   turbine.
10. [021.11.01.02.10] -- Define the term "bypass ratio" and perform simple
    calculations to determine it.
11. [021.11.01.02.11] -- Define the terms "propulsive power", "propulsive
    efficiency", "thermal efficiency" and "total efficiency".
12. [021.11.01.02.12] -- Describe the influence of compressor-pressure ratio on
    thermal efficiency.
13. [021.11.01.02.13] -- Explain the variations of propulsive efficiency with
    forward speed for turbojet, turbofan and turboprop engines.
14. [021.11.01.02.14] -- Define the term "specific fuel consumption" for
    turbojets and turboprops.

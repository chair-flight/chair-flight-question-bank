# General

## Learning Objectives

1. **021.11.04.01.01** -- Explain the following aeroplane engine ratings:
   take-off; go-around; maximum continuous thrust/power; maximum climb
   thrust/power.
2. **021.11.04.01.02** -- Explain spool-up time.
3. **021.11.04.01.03** -- Explain the reason for the difference between ground and
   approach flight idle values (rpm).
4. **021.11.04.01.04** -- State the parameters that can be used for setting and
   monitoring the thrust/power.
5. **021.11.04.01.05** -- Describe the terms "alpha range", "beta range" and
   "reverse thrust" as applied to a turboprop power lever.
6. **021.11.04.01.06** -- Explain the dangers of inadvertent beta-range selection
   in flight for a turboprop.
7. **021.11.04.01.07** -- Explain the purpose of engine trending.
8. **021.11.04.01.08** -- Explain how the exhaust gas temperature is used to
   monitor turbine stress.
9. **021.11.04.01.09** -- Describe the effect of engine acceleration and
   deceleration on the EGT.
10. **021.11.04.01.10** -- Describe the possible effects on engine components when
    EGT limits are exceeded.
11. **021.11.04.01.11** -- Explain why engine-limit exceedances must be reported.
12. **021.11.04.01.12** -- Explain the limitations on the use of the
    thrust-reverser system at low forward speed.
13. **021.11.04.01.13** -- Explain the term "engine seizure".
14. **021.11.04.01.14** -- State the possible causes of engine seizure and explain
    their preventative measures.
15. **021.11.04.01.15** -- Describe the potential consequences of a leak in the
    following two designs of fuel and oil heat exchanger: oil pressure higher
    than fuel pressure with oil leaking into the fuel system, potentially
    affecting the combustion and running of the engine; fuel pressure higher
    than oil pressure with fuel leaking into the oil system, potentially
    increasing the risk of a fire due to fuel entering warm parts of the engine
    that should be free from fuel.
16. **021.11.04.01.16** -- Explain oil-filter clogging (blockage) and the
    implications for the lubrication system.
17. **021.11.04.01.17** -- Give examples of monitoring instruments of an engine.
18. **021.11.04.01.18** -- Describe how to identify and assess engine damage based
    on instrument indications.

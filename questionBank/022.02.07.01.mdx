# Operating principle, display, CAS, TAS and Mach number

## Learning Objectives

1. **022.02.07.01.01** -- Define "Mach number" and "local speed sound" (LSS).
   Calculate between LSS, TAS and Mach number.
2. **022.02.07.01.02** -- Describe the operating principle of a Machmeter.
3. **022.02.07.01.03** -- Explain why a Machmeter does not suffer from
   compressibility error.
4. **022.02.07.01.04** -- Give examples of a Machmeter display: pointer, drum,
   vertical straight scale, digital.
5. **022.02.07.01.05** -- Describe the effects on a Machmeter of a blockage or a
   leakage in the static or total pressure line(s).
6. **022.02.07.01.06** -- Explain the relationship between CAS, TAS and Mach
   number. Explain how CAS, TAS and Mach number vary in relation to each other
   during a climb, a descent, or in level flight in different temperature
   conditions.
7. **022.02.07.01.07** -- State the existence of maximum operating limit speed
   (VMO) and maximum operating Mach number (MMO).
8. **022.02.07.01.08** -- Describe typical indications of MMO and VMO on
   analogue and digital instruments.
9. **022.02.07.01.09** -- Describe the relationship between MMO and VMO with
   change in altitude and the implications of climbing at constant IAS and
   descending at constant Mach number with respect to the margin to MMO and VMO.
10. **022.02.07.01.10** -- Describe the implications of climbing or descending
    at constant Mach number or constant IAS with respect to the margin to the
    stall speed or maximum speed.

# Lambert

## Learning Objectives

1. **061.04.02.04.01** -- State the properties of a Lambert projection.
2. **061.04.02.04.02** -- Calculate straight line track changes on a Lambert
   chart.
3. **061.04.02.04.03** -- Explain the scale variation throughout the charts as
   follows: the scale indicated on the chart will be correct at the standard
   parallels; the scale will increase away from the parallel of origin; the
   scale within the standard parallels differs by less than 1Â per cent from the
   scale stated on the chart.
4. **061.04.02.04.04** -- Given appropriate data, calculate initial, final or
   rhumb-line tracks between two positions (lat./long.).
5. **061.04.02.04.05** -- Given two positions (lat./long.) and information to
   determine convergency between the two positions, calculate the parallel of
   origin.
6. **061.04.02.04.06** -- Given a Lambert chart, determine the parallel of
   origin, or constant of cone.
7. **061.04.02.04.07** -- Given constant of cone or parallel of origin,
   great-circle track at one position and great-circle track at another
   position, calculate the difference of longitude between the two positions.
